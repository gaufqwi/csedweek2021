Wednesday Trivia

Give these trivia questions a try. Click the blurry text to see the answers.

  1. December 9 is the birthday of what pioneering computer scientist who served as a rear admiral in the United States Navy? She was also instrumental in the creation of the COBOL programming language.

    Answer: Grace Hopper

    In 1947 Hopper and her team discovered a moth lodged in one of the components of the computer they were using, preventing it from working correctly. For that reason she is often credited with coining the word "debugging" to mean finding and fixing the errors in a computer system, though in all likelihood the word was in use before this incident.

  2. Lots of irritating single parentheses are a feature of what list processing language that is an ancestor of both LOGO and Scheme?

    Answer: Lisp

    Layers and layers of nested parentheses are a distinctive feature of Lisp. Here is typical example of Lisp code:

    
(defun factorial (n)
    (if (= n 0) 1
        (* n (factorial (- n 1)))))
  3. Computers can represent numbers several different ways. In the binary system numbers are represented using powers of 2. Another system, hexadecimal, uses powers of what number?

    Answer: 16

    Hexadecimal needs 16 different digits, so 0 - 9 are used as usual and the letters A - F are used to represent the quantities 10 - 15. For example, the hexadecimal value 3C equals 60 since the 3 means three 16s and the C means twelve 1s, and 3 × 16 + 12 × 1 = 60.

  4. The Cascading Style Sheet (CSS) code below will make a web page with what color background? 
    
body {
    background-color: rgb(0, 0, 255);
}

    Answer: blue

    The 'r', 'g', and 'b' in rgb() stand for red, green, and blue. rgb(0, 0, 255) means a color that is all blue with no red or green. rgb(255, 0, 255) would be purple (actually pinkish purple) since that's what you get when you mix red and blue.
