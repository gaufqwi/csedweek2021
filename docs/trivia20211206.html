Monday Trivia

Give these trivia questions a try. Click the blurry text to see the answers.

  1. The Gopher protocol was a precursor to the World Wide Web. At what American university was Gopher invented.

    Answer: University of Minnesota

    The protocol was named for the the University of Minnesota's mascot, the Golden Gophers. The Gopher protocol was menu based and designed primarily for text based terminals and was mostly made obsolete the multimedia capabilities of the web.

  2. BASIC was the first computer language of many programmers in the 1970s and 1980s. What does the the 'B' in BASIC stand for?

    Answer: Beginners'

    BASIC stands for Beginners' All Purpose Symbolic Instruction Code. It was designed by John G. Kemeny and Thomas E. Kurtz and released at Dartmouth College in 1964.

  3. One of the first ever hand held computers was released by Apple in 1993 and named for what physicist and mathematician?

    Answer: Sir Isaac Newton

    The phrase "personal digital assistant" (PDA) was first used to describe the Apple Newton. Even though the Newton had some advanced features like handwriting recognition, it never really took off. Still, in many ways the Newton paved the way for devices like the smart phones and tablets.

  4. What number should replace the XX so this LOGO code will instruct the turtle to draw a hexagon? 
    
repeat 6 [fd 50 rt XX]

    Answer: 60

    When drawing a closed figure, the turtle needs to make a complete turn or 360° Since a hexagon has six sides, that means at each corner the turtle must turn 360 ÷ 6 or 60°
